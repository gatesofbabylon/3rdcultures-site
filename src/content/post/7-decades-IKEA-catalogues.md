Actually pretty remarkable that IKEA’s [catalogues](https://ikeamuseum.com/sv/ikea-kataloger/) become instantly recognisable in content in only their 12th year of existence. If that’s not a mark of a strong visual identity, I don’t know what is.

![](/images/posts/7-decades-IKEA-catalogues/IMG_4841.jpeg)

#### The 1961 edition.

IKEA catalogue from 1961 – the twelfth edition – is the first that is instantly identifiable as such. It set down a template for future catalogues.

![](/images/posts/7-decades-IKEA-catalogues/IMG_4842.jpeg)

#### The beforetimes.

The first six editions of the IKEA catalogue, from 1950 through 1955: trippy and abstract; monochrome.

![](/images/posts/7-decades-IKEA-catalogues/IMG_4843.jpeg)

#### Post-1961.

The eight editions following 1961, from 1962 through 1969: already iconic.
